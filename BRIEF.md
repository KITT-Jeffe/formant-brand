# Formant Brand Brief
*March 2026 — Working document*

---

## The Company

Formant builds AI agents for physical operations. Not digital automation — the real world. Machines, fleets, field technicians, industrial systems. The thesis: physical operations have been untouched by the AI revolution. Until now.

- **formant.io** — legacy platform site (fleet management, robotics ops, enterprise SaaS)
- **formant.ai** — new direction, austere, declarative, "Physical AI is here"

The `.ai` site has the right posture. It needs visual gravity.

---

## The Name

"Formant" comes from acoustics. Formants are the resonant frequencies that give a human voice its unique character — the signal extracted from noise that makes a voice *identifiable*. This is the company: finding the meaningful signal in physical operations before it becomes a problem.

Worth leaning into. Not loudly — as an undercurrent.

---

## Brand Idea

**Signal in the physical world.**

Formant sees what others miss. The anomaly before the failure. The pattern before the incident. There's something almost uncanny about it — a machine that reads environments the way an experienced field engineer reads a room. That capability should feel earned, quiet, and slightly mysterious. Not magic. Mastery.

People should be *intrigued* by what Formant does. Not just impressed. Intrigued.

## Visual Direction

### Photography
- Hasselblad 500C/M · Kodak Tri-X 400 as the signature aesthetic
- Machines shot like portraits — with gravity and specificity
- High contrast, visible grain, photographic texture
- B&W primary; color used sparingly and purposefully
- Square or cinematic crops (not ad-agency widescreen)
- Subject matter: robots, industrial equipment, field operations, the human-machine interface
- Tone: documentary, not promotional

### Color
- **Foundation:** Near-black + white. Photographic, not UI dark mode.
- **Accent:** Warm near-red — signal color, not danger color. Industrial. Present.
- Not blue. Everyone in robotics/AI is blue.

### Typography
- Geometric grotesque for body + UI (Swiss precision, Rams-adjacent)
- Heavier display weight for headlines — presence, not decoration
- Monospace face for data contexts (readouts, telemetry, system states)
- Tight tracking. Generous leading. Nothing decorative.

### Layout
- Rams-level rigor: grid-locked, everything justified by function
- MIT Media Lab systematicity — the system is the identity
- Teenage Engineering restraint — obvious only in retrospect

### Logo
- The existing F-mark (geometric shapes, no curves) has bones — don't discard it
- Its constructivist, grid-built geometry is right; it just lacks the conceptual layer
- Direction: keep the rigid constructed-shapes DNA, introduce the waveform as the one element that breaks the geometry — a single signal line through an otherwise perfectly ordered form
- The anomaly, literally built into the mark. Total order + one disruption = the brand idea made visible
- Must work at 16px and on a hardware label
- Explored directions: v1 (grid F + spike), v3 "F Signal Study" (bold F + waveform cut) — both arrived at same idea independently

## Tone of Voice

- Declarative, not explanatory
- Confident without being loud
- Slightly withholding — doesn't over-explain
- The mystery isn't vagueness; it's *precision that implies depth*
- "We know something you don't yet. Come find out."

---

## References

- **Teenage Engineering** — Scandinavian restraint, exposed function as beauty, mono type, bold color pops against neutral
- **Dieter Rams / Braun** — "Less but better." Every element earns its place. Honesty of materials.
- **MIT Media Lab (Pentagram)** — Systematic identity, the grid *is* the brand, scalable across contexts
- **Hasselblad/Tri-X photography** — Grain as texture, not artifact. Machines as subjects worthy of portraiture.
- **Vehicle Collection PDF** — The aesthetic achieved there (cinematic, spare, photographic) is the north star
